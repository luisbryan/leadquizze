Right On Track

Congratulations! You have not succumb to the pitfalls of overtraining, or undertraining and you know what it takes to get the results you desire!

You know the importance of mixing up your routine and are not afraid to take a day of rest. When someone says they work out two times a day every day without fail, you just sit back and smile knowing that you are getting the results you want and have a life outside of the gym.

It looks like you may be getting into the undertraining syndrome. You’ll need some flexing of muscles and some determination in order to get anywhere close to being fit. Doing regular exercise is commendable in that it helps with better blood circulation and maintaining a particular level of muscle mass and strength and metabolism. However, once it gets into a steady state, even if for a prolonged period, it halts any further benefit such as increased muscle mass or strength. This is because the body has gotten accustomed to that level of workout.
In order to achieve the shape, mass, volume and strength of muscle and physique that you seek, you need to increase the intensity of your workout. You must, however, watch over getting your results without getting into the extremes as you might get into overtraining which carries with it many problems such as increased cortisol hormone production which results to increased levels of stored fat and decreased production of the growth hormone which also increases levels of fat in the body, as well as, weakens muscles and bones.
You must then know how to strike the balance between undertraining and overtraining to achieve that fitness state, body shape or muscle mass that you are aiming to have.
